Ingredients

  1. 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, or more as needed (Do not over oil this -IP)
  2. 1 ¾ pounds beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
  3. 3 onions, quartered (Fewer onions is fine. You won't miss them. You won't miss the onions. -IP)
  4. 1 tablespoon ketchup (The ketchup helps with it having a ketchupy twang. I have honestly never heard of ketchup in a recipe but this person knows more than me. -IP)
  5. 1 ½ teaspoons Worcestershire sauce (I had to copy-paste this because I couldn't spell it -IP)
  6. 1 pinch cayenne pepper, or to taste (Optional) (Okay, fine. -IP)
  7. water to cover
  8. 4 carrots, cut into 2-inch pieces (The carrots you will miss. Do not skimp on the carrots. -IP)
  9. 1 cube chicken bouillon (Optional) (Do not. -IP)
  10. 3 medium potatoes, cut into 3-inch chunks (Potato that shit up, fam. -IP)
  11. 1 ½ (3.5 ounce) containers Japanese curry roux, or more to taste. (more, because of the taste -IP)

Now you've got a bunch of stuff! Congratulations on going to the store. Here's the rest of the recipe. Like... the making it part.

The Making It Part


  • Heat oil in a 6-quart pot over medium-high heat. Add beef and saute until brown, 5 to 7 minutes. Add onions and cook until starting to soften, about 3 minutes. Add ketchup and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to coat. Add cayenne pepper. Pour in water to cover mixture by 1 or 2 inches. Add carrots and bouillon.

  • Simmer, skimming fat off the surface of the broth as needed, for 30 minutes. Add potatoes. Stir in 1 package of curry roux and let dissolve; add remaining curry as needed to achieve desired thickness. Continue simmering until beef and vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more.
